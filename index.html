Frequently Asked Questions

Who can participate?

Any high schooler can participate, if you are starting ninth grade in the fall, you can participate too!

What If I don’t know how to code?

No problem! One of the main points of a hackathon is to help teach students who have no prior coding experience, helping them create something great from scratch. We will have mentors and workshops to help you all night and day. All we ask from you is the willingness to learn and you can certainly create something you can be proud of.

How much does this cost?

Short answer: nothing. Long answer: $0.

What should I bring to the hackathon?

  • A Laptop + Charger
  • Cellphone and Mobile Devices + Chargers
  • Sleeping Bag (Highly Recommended)
  • At least two sets of clothes
  • Basic Toiletries

I don't have a team!

We'll have a team building session right before hacking starts. We'll quickly introduce ourselves and form teams however we choose. You'll make cool new friends!

What do I make?

Anything you'd like. Come up with your favorite idea and make it a reality! Use any programming languages, development stacks, APIs, and other tools you need.

Can I continue my awesome project I've been working on?

Thats awesome your already hacking away. Unfortunately, all code for this event must be written during hacking window during the event, but use of public, open-source external libraries and APIs are encouraged!

Do I need to be at the venue at all times?

Due to security reasons, you must stay in the building at all times. If you have an emergency or really have to leave, don't worry. We'll work it out.

I have some dietary restrictions.

We've got you covered. Just mention it in the application (coming soon.)

I'm not a high school student.

Come in as a mentor to help out! Mentors with any skills related to creating products (programming, design, UX, buisiness) are welcome to help out. Mentor sign-ups will be open soon.

Did we miss your question?

You can contact us at team(at)hackdc.org and we'll do our best to answer your question!
